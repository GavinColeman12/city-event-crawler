# Horizon Technologies — Performance Review Process

## Overview
Horizon runs a continuous feedback culture supported by formal review cycles. Performance reviews directly inform compensation adjustments, bonuses, and career development.

## Review Cycle

### Quarterly Check-ins
- Informal 30-minute conversation between employee and manager
- Discuss: progress on goals, obstacles, development needs, feedback
- No formal rating — focused on alignment and support
- Documented briefly in BambooHR by the manager

### Annual Performance Review (March)
- Comprehensive review covering the prior calendar year
- Self-assessment submitted by employee (due February 15)
- Manager assessment with ratings
- Calibration across teams by leadership (first 2 weeks of March)
- Review conversation between manager and employee (second 2 weeks of March)
- Compensation adjustments effective April 1

## Rating Scale

| Rating | Definition |
|--------|-----------|
| Exceptional (5) | Consistently exceeds expectations in all areas. Demonstrates leadership and impact well beyond role scope. Top 5–10% of performers. |
| Exceeds Expectations (4) | Frequently exceeds expectations. Delivers high-quality work and contributes beyond core responsibilities. |
| Meets Expectations (3) | Consistently meets all expectations for the role. Solid, reliable performance. This is a good rating — most employees fall here. |
| Needs Improvement (2) | Meets some but not all expectations. Specific areas require development. PIP may be initiated. |
| Below Expectations (1) | Does not meet expectations in key areas. PIP will be initiated with clear timeline for improvement. |

## Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
If an employee receives a rating of 2 or below, or if performance concerns arise at any time:

1. Manager documents specific performance gaps and meets with employee and HR
2. Written PIP outlines: specific expectations, measurable goals, timeline (typically 30–60 days), support provided, and consequences of not meeting goals
3. Weekly check-ins during PIP period
4. At end of PIP: employee either meets expectations (PIP closed) or does not (termination or extended PIP at HR's discretion)

PIPs are not punishment — they are a structured support mechanism. Many employees successfully complete PIPs and go on to strong performance.

## Promotion Criteria
Promotions at Horizon are based on:
- Sustained performance at or above the next level for 6+ months
- Demonstration of skills and behaviors expected at the next level
- Business need for the role at the next level
- Manager recommendation and leadership approval

Promotions are typically announced in April following the annual review cycle but may occur mid-year for exceptional circumstances.
