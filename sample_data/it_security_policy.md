# Horizon Technologies — IT Security Policy

## Overview
Protecting Horizon's data, systems, and client information is everyone's responsibility. This policy outlines the security practices all employees must follow.

## Passwords and Authentication
- All accounts must use passwords with minimum 12 characters, including uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for all company systems — no exceptions
- Never share your passwords with anyone, including IT staff (IT will never ask for your password)
- Use the company-provided password manager (1Password) for all work-related passwords
- Change passwords immediately if you suspect compromise

## Device Security
- All company devices must have full-disk encryption enabled
- Install software updates within 48 hours of notification
- Lock your screen when stepping away from your device (Cmd+L on Mac, Win+L on Windows)
- Do not install unauthorized software on company devices — submit requests through IT
- Report lost or stolen devices to IT immediately (it-security@horizontech.com) — we can remotely wipe within minutes

## Data Classification
- **Public**: Marketing materials, published blog posts, open-source code
- **Internal**: Internal communications, project plans, financial summaries
- **Confidential**: Client data, source code, employee records, contracts, strategic plans
- **Restricted**: Authentication credentials, encryption keys, security audit reports

Never send Confidential or Restricted data via personal email, unapproved cloud storage, or messaging apps. Use approved channels only (company email, Google Drive, approved Slack channels).

## Network Security
- Use the company VPN when working outside the office or on public WiFi
- Do not connect to public WiFi without VPN active
- Do not use personal devices for accessing company systems unless enrolled in MDM (Mobile Device Management)
- Report suspicious emails (phishing) by forwarding to phishing@horizontech.com

## Incident Reporting
If you suspect a security incident (unauthorized access, data breach, lost device, phishing compromise):
1. Do not attempt to investigate yourself
2. Contact IT Security immediately: it-security@horizontech.com or extension 300
3. Do not discuss the incident with anyone outside the response team
4. Preserve any evidence (don't delete emails, don't wipe devices)

## Acceptable Use
Company devices and networks are for business use. Limited personal use is permitted but:
- Do not access illegal, offensive, or inappropriate content
- Do not use company systems for personal business or side projects
- Do not store personal files on company systems
- Company reserves the right to monitor network traffic and device usage

## Training
All employees must complete annual security awareness training (administered through KnowBe4). New employees must complete training within their first 2 weeks. Failure to complete required training may result in system access suspension.

## Questions
Contact IT Security at it-security@horizontech.com or extension 300.
