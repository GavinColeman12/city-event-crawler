# Horizon Technologies — Code of Conduct

## Our Values
Horizon Technologies is built on four core values that guide our behavior:

1. **Integrity**: We do the right thing, even when no one is watching
2. **Collaboration**: We achieve more together than alone
3. **Innovation**: We challenge assumptions and embrace change
4. **Respect**: We treat every person with dignity and professionalism

## Professional Conduct

### Expected Behavior
- Treat all colleagues, clients, and partners with respect and professionalism
- Communicate openly, honestly, and constructively
- Take ownership of your work and commitments
- Support your teammates and celebrate shared wins
- Raise concerns through appropriate channels rather than gossip or passive aggression
- Protect confidential information — both the company's and our clients'
- Represent Horizon positively in all professional and public interactions

### Unacceptable Behavior
The following are grounds for disciplinary action up to and including immediate termination:
- Harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any kind
- Theft, fraud, or dishonesty
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information
- Workplace violence or threats of violence
- Possession of weapons on company premises
- Being under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs during work hours
- Insubordination or refusal to perform assigned duties
- Falsification of company records, timesheets, or expense reports
- Unauthorized use of company resources for personal gain

## Confidentiality

### Company Information
Employees have access to confidential information including business strategies, financial data, client information, product roadmaps, and employee data. This information must not be shared with anyone outside the company without explicit authorization.

### Client Information
Client data and project details are strictly confidential. Never discuss client work in public settings, on social media, or with employees who are not on the same project without client consent.

### NDA
All employees sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) as a condition of employment. The NDA remains in effect for 2 years after separation from the company.

## Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience a violation of this Code of Conduct:

1. **Talk to your manager** — for most issues, your direct manager is the first point of contact
2. **Contact HR** — hr@horizontech.com or extension 200, for sensitive issues or if your manager is involved
3. **Anonymous Hotline** — 1-800-555-ETHICS, for situations where you want to report anonymously
4. **CEO Direct Line** — ceo@horizontech.com, for matters of extreme concern

All reports are investigated promptly. Horizon strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith. Retaliation itself is grounds for termination.

## Disciplinary Process

Horizon follows a progressive discipline process for most policy violations:

1. **Verbal Warning**: Documented conversation with manager
2. **Written Warning**: Formal written notice with specific expectations and timeline for improvement
3. **Final Written Warning / Performance Improvement Plan**: Last chance with clear consequences for non-improvement
4. **Termination**: If previous steps haven't resolved the issue

Severe violations (violence, theft, fraud, gross misconduct) may result in immediate termination without progressive discipline.

## Questions
If you're unsure whether an action complies with our Code of Conduct, ask yourself:
- Is it legal?
- Does it align with our values?
- Would I be comfortable if it were reported in the news?
- Would I be comfortable explaining it to my manager?

If the answer to any of these is "no" or "I'm not sure," stop and ask HR before proceeding.
