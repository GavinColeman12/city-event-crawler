# Horizon Technologies — Employee Handbook
**Effective Date: January 1, 2026**
**Last Updated: March 15, 2026**

## Welcome to Horizon Technologies

Welcome to Horizon Technologies! We're a team of 85 people building enterprise workflow automation software from our headquarters in Austin, TX and our satellite office in Denver, CO. This handbook outlines the policies, benefits, and expectations that make Horizon a great place to work. Please read it thoroughly and reach out to HR (hr@horizontech.com) with any questions.

## Employment Basics

### At-Will Employment
Employment at Horizon Technologies is at-will, meaning either you or the company may end the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice. No provision in this handbook constitutes a contract of employment or a guarantee of continued employment.

### Equal Employment Opportunity
Horizon Technologies is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, veteran status, or any other legally protected characteristic. This policy applies to all employment practices including hiring, promotion, compensation, training, and termination.

### Employment Classifications
- **Full-Time**: Regularly scheduled for 30+ hours per week. Eligible for all benefits.
- **Part-Time**: Regularly scheduled for fewer than 30 hours per week. Eligible for limited benefits as outlined in the Benefits Guide.
- **Contract**: Engaged for a specific project or time period. Not eligible for benefits unless specified in contract.
- **Intern**: Temporary position for learning purposes. May be paid or unpaid per applicable law.

### Introductory Period
All new employees undergo a 90-day introductory period. During this time, your manager will provide regular feedback and a formal 90-day review. Successful completion of the introductory period is required for continued employment but does not change the at-will nature of employment. During the introductory period, PTO accrues but cannot be used until after the 90-day mark.

### Background Checks
All offers of employment are contingent upon successful completion of a background check, which may include criminal history, employment verification, education verification, and credit history (for positions with financial responsibilities).

## Compensation

### Pay Schedule
Horizon Technologies pays employees on a semi-monthly basis, on the 15th and last day of each month. If a pay date falls on a weekend or holiday, payment will be made on the preceding business day.

### Direct Deposit
Direct deposit is required for all employees. Please provide your banking information to HR within your first week. Changes to banking information require 5 business days to process.

### Overtime
Non-exempt employees are eligible for overtime pay at 1.5x their regular hourly rate for hours worked in excess of 40 per workweek. All overtime must be pre-approved by your manager. Unauthorized overtime may result in disciplinary action.

### Annual Raises
Horizon conducts annual compensation reviews in March. Raises are based on individual performance, market benchmarks, and company financial performance. The typical raise range is 3–8% for employees meeting or exceeding expectations. Raises are not guaranteed.

### Bonuses
Horizon offers discretionary annual bonuses based on company performance and individual contribution. Bonus targets vary by level:
- Individual Contributor (IC1–IC3): 5–10% of base salary
- Senior Individual Contributor (IC4–IC5): 10–15% of base salary
- Manager: 15–20% of base salary
- Director+: 20–30% of base salary

Bonuses are typically paid in Q1 for the prior year's performance. Employees must be employed on the bonus payout date to receive a bonus.

## Working Hours and Attendance

### Standard Working Hours
Horizon's standard business hours are 9:00 AM to 5:30 PM local time, Monday through Friday. However, we recognize that flexibility matters. Employees may adjust their schedule with manager approval, provided they maintain core hours of 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM local time for collaboration and are available for scheduled meetings.

### Time Tracking
Non-exempt employees must track their hours using Harvest (our time tracking tool). Timesheets are due by end of day each Friday. Exempt employees do not track hours but are expected to maintain a schedule that meets business needs.

### Attendance
Regular attendance is essential. If you will be absent or late, notify your manager as soon as possible, preferably before your shift begins. Three or more unexcused absences within a 90-day rolling period may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

## Workplace Policies

### Dress Code
Horizon maintains a business casual dress code. This means:
- **Acceptable**: Jeans (no rips), collared shirts, blouses, sweaters, clean sneakers, dresses, skirts (knee-length or longer)
- **Not acceptable**: Athletic wear (gym shorts, sweatpants, tank tops), flip-flops, clothing with offensive graphics, excessively torn clothing
- **Client meetings**: Business professional attire required when meeting clients in person
- **Fridays**: Casual Fridays — relaxed dress code, including company t-shirts and clean athletic shoes
Managers may set additional standards for their teams based on client-facing requirements.

### Workplace Safety
Horizon is committed to providing a safe work environment. Report any unsafe conditions to Facilities (facilities@horizontech.com) immediately. In case of emergency, call 911 first, then notify your manager and HR.

### Drug and Alcohol Policy
Horizon maintains a drug-free workplace. The use, possession, sale, or distribution of illegal drugs on company premises or during work hours is prohibited and grounds for immediate termination. Alcohol may be served at company events at management's discretion but excessive consumption is prohibited. If you believe you have a substance abuse problem, please contact HR confidentially for assistance through our EAP.

### Harassment and Discrimination
Horizon has zero tolerance for harassment, discrimination, or retaliation of any kind. This includes but is not limited to: sexual harassment, racial harassment, bullying, intimidation, and creating a hostile work environment. If you experience or witness harassment, report it immediately to HR, your manager, or any member of leadership. All reports will be investigated promptly and confidentially. Retaliation against anyone who reports harassment is strictly prohibited and will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

### Conflicts of Interest
Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with the interests of Horizon Technologies. This includes but is not limited to: outside employment that competes with or interferes with your role, financial interests in competitors or vendors, and hiring or supervising family members. Disclose any potential conflicts to HR in writing.

### Social Media Policy
Employees are free to use social media personally. However:
- Do not share confidential company information, client information, or trade secrets
- Do not represent personal opinions as Horizon's official position
- Do not disparage the company, colleagues, clients, or competitors
- If you identify yourself as a Horizon employee, include a disclaimer that views are your own
Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary action.

## Separation of Employment

### Voluntary Resignation
Employees who wish to resign are asked to provide at least 2 weeks written notice to their manager and HR. Longer notice is appreciated for senior roles. Resignation should be submitted in writing (email is acceptable) to your manager with a copy to hr@horizontech.com.

### Involuntary Termination
Horizon may terminate employment at any time for any reason consistent with at-will employment. Common grounds for termination include but are not limited to: performance issues not resolved through the performance improvement process, violation of company policies, misconduct, insubordination, and business restructuring.

### Exit Process
Departing employees will:
1. Participate in an exit interview with HR
2. Return all company property (laptop, badges, keys, equipment)
3. Complete a knowledge transfer with their manager
4. Have final paycheck processed per state law (Texas: next regular pay date)
5. Receive COBRA information for benefits continuation within 14 days of termination

### Final Paycheck
Accrued but unused PTO will be paid out in the final paycheck for employees who have completed the 90-day introductory period. Company-specific holidays and floating holidays are not paid out upon separation.
